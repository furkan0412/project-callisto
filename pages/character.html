Best Stargate Characters Ever

Stargate introduces us many characters but some of them become like a family. We are take a quick look at some of these wonderful characters together in this section. Some characters are good and some are evil but They all effect us in a way.

Jonathan J. O'Neill

He's an amazing man. After everything he's done, he's still modest, quite self effacing actually. He even likes people to think he's not as smart as he really is. Bottom line: He's an incredibly strong leader who's given more of himself for this program than anyone has given for... well... anything I can imagine.

―Samantha Carter, Stargate SG-1

Jonathan "Jack" O'Neill is one of the most iconic and beloved characters in the Stargate franchise. Portrayed by Richard Dean Anderson, O'Neill is known for his dry wit, tactical brilliance, and a deeply human approach to leadership. As the commanding officer of the original SG-1 team, he played a central role in the early missions through the Stargate, often balancing military objectives with moral integrity and compassion.

Jack O'Neill is a decorated U.S. Air Force officer with a background in black ops and deep-space telemetry. Before joining the Stargate Program, O'Neill had retired from active duty following the tragic loss of his young son, Charlie — an event that left him emotionally distant and introspective. His involvement in the original Stargate mission to Abydos in the 1994 Stargate film was meant to be a one-way trip. But the mission's success — and his bond with the alien culture — gave him a renewed sense of purpose.

When Stargate Command was reactivated, O'Neill was brought back to lead SG-1, the flagship team tasked with exploring new worlds, defending Earth, and confronting threats like the Goa'uld and later the Replicators and Ori. While O'Neill maintained a no-nonsense military exterior, his loyalty to his team — Samantha Carter, Daniel Jackson, and Teal'c — became one of the emotional pillars of the series.

O'Neill's leadership style is uniquely his own. He is often sarcastic, irreverent, and even dismissive of complex scientific jargon (much to Daniel and Carter's frustration), but beneath the humor is a deeply strategic thinker and a courageous leader who repeatedly risks his life for his team and for Earth. He isn't afraid to break the rules when necessary — whether it's defying orders to save a life or standing up to alien gods — and he brings a humanitarian touch to the often cold world of military command. His respect for alien cultures, especially his enduring friendship with Teal'c, shows his capacity for empathy and growth.

Final Word

O'Neill eventually rises to the rank of Brigadier General, taking command of Stargate Command before later moving to a more strategic role in Homeworld Security. His influence continues to shape the Stargate Program even after he steps back from active field duty. He represents the soul of SG-1: skeptical but open-minded, tough but compassionate, sarcastic but principled. Jack O'Neill isn't just a soldier — he's the beating heart of Stargate SG-1 and a fan-favorite character whose impact continues to echo across the Stargate universe.

Samantha Carter

Carter, you're one of this country's natural resources, if not national treasures.

―Jonathan J. O'Neill, Stargate SG-1

Colonel Samantha Carter is one of the most accomplished and multidimensional characters in the Stargate universe. Portrayed by Amanda Tapping, Carter is a brilliant astrophysicist, decorated Air Force officer, and key member of the legendary SG-1 team. Balancing intellect, leadership, and compassion, she represents the perfect fusion of science and military discipline in the Stargate program.

Samantha Carter joined the U.S. Air Force at a young age, driven by a passion for astrophysics and aerospace engineering. She earned a Ph.D. in theoretical astrophysics, worked on top-secret research including the Stargate project before its public reactivation, and served in the Gulf War as a combat pilot. By the time she joins SG-1, Carter has already logged over 100 hours in enemy airspace — a fact she reminds O'Neill of during their first mission briefing.

Carter's role in SG-1 is essential. She serves as the science officer, tasked with understanding and manipulating alien technology, including the Stargate itself. But she's far more than a scientist. She's a battle-tested soldier, trained for field combat, capable of tactical decisions under pressure, and one of the team's most dependable operators.

Over the years, Carter plays a vital role in:

  • Defeating the Goa'uld system lords through scientific innovation.
  • Battling and outsmarting the Replicators, including her evil mirror-version, Replicator Carter.
  • Developing Earth's interstellar defense capabilities, including the BC-303 and Daedalus-class ships.
  • Rising to command SG-1 and later the Atlantis Expedition.

Carter is highly analytical, logical, and precise — but she's also deeply empathetic and loyal. She's constantly torn between her scientific curiosity and her military duty, which leads to complex moral dilemmas throughout the series. Her humility, despite being arguably one of Earth's smartest people, is one of her most grounding traits. As a leader, Carter is fair, strategic, and firm when needed. Whether she's commanding SG-1, helping lead the fight against the Ori, or taking charge of Stargate Atlantis, she proves time and again that she can lead with strength, grace, and intelligence.

Her professional bond with Jack O'Neill evolves into a deep — but unspoken — romantic tension, complicated by military regulations. Her relationships with Daniel and Teal'c are built on mutual respect and friendship, forming a family dynamic within SG-1.

Carter's legacy is immense:

  • She helps reverse-engineer Goa'uld and Ancient tech.
  • Plays a vital role in developing Earth's intergalactic space fleet.
  • Acts as a scientific and military ambassador in both the Milky Way and Pegasus galaxies.

Final Word

Colonel Samantha Carter is a trailblazer, not just within the Stargate universe, but in science fiction television as a whole. She's one of the rare characters who can reroute a wormhole, disarm a nuclear bomb, and win a gunfight — all in the same episode. She stands as a symbol of brilliance, bravery, and balance — a hero who proves that being smart doesn't mean being weak, and that leadership comes in many forms. Carter is, without question, one of Stargate's greatest legends.

Daniel Jackson

You are... brilliant. One of the most caring, passionate... you're the type of person who would give his own life for someone he doesn't even know. If you had one fault, it was that you wanted to save people so badly, y-you wanted to help people so much, that it tore you apart when you couldn't make a difference.

―Samantha Carter, Stargate SG-1

Dr. Daniel Jackson is the heart and soul of Stargate SG-1 — a passionate archaeologist, linguist, and humanitarian who helps unlock the secrets of the universe. Portrayed by Michael Shanks, Daniel begins as a brilliant outsider ridiculed for his theories, only to become one of the most essential figures in Earth's fight against galactic threats.

Dr. Jackson holds multiple doctorates, including archaeology, anthropology, and linguistics. Fluent in more than 20 Earth languages and a growing number of alien ones, Daniel was dismissed by the academic community for his theory that the Great Pyramids of Egypt were far older than believed and built with alien influence. Ironically, he turned out to be absolutely right.Dr. Jackson holds multiple doctorates, including archaeology, anthropology, and linguistics. Fluent in more than 20 Earth languages and a growing number of alien ones, Daniel was dismissed by the academic community for his theory that the Great Pyramids of Egypt were far older than believed and built with alien influence. Ironically, he turned out to be absolutely right.

Daniel joined SG-1 not as a soldier, but as an academic — yet he quickly proved himself as a capable and courageous field agent. He serves as the team's expert on:

  • Alien cultures
  • Dead languages and written systems
  • Ancient technology and mythology
  • Diplomatic first contact protocols

He's often the moral compass of SG-1, challenging militaristic thinking and pushing for diplomacy, understanding, and compassion — even when surrounded by danger. While O'Neill brings the guns, and Carter brings the science, Daniel brings wisdom.

  • Loss of Sha're to the Goa'uld Apophis shapes his early motivations.
  • Ascension (becoming a being of pure energy) elevates him to a near-omnipotent state, where he learns the deeper truths of the universe — and the limits of cosmic detachment.
  • He returns to human form with greater insight and resolve, continuing to fight the Goa'uld, Replicators, and Ori with renewed perspective.

His transformation from idealistic scholar to intergalactic warrior-philosopher is one of Stargate's most compelling journeys.

Daniel is curious, stubborn, and fiercely principled. He is often in direct contrast with O'Neill's pragmatism or the Air Force's chain of command, but that tension is what gives SG-1 balance.

He's unafraid to:

  • Speak out against morally questionable decisions
  • Stand up to both enemies and allies when he sees injustice
  • Risk everything to save a life or preserve a culture

Daniel believes knowledge is sacred, that life has value, and that humanity must rise above its destructive instincts.

Legacy

Daniel Jackson is responsible for:

  • Unlocking the Stargate and launching the entire program
  • Translating the Ancient language and discovering Earth's connection to the Ancients/Lanteans
  • Understanding the mythology and function of Ascension
  • Negotiating countless peace treaties and cultural exchanges across the galaxy

He is Earth's first and greatest explorer of the stars — not by weapon, but by word.

Final Word

Dr. Daniel Jackson embodies the soul of exploration: not just reaching new worlds, but understanding them. He reminds us that knowledge is power — not to dominate, but to uplift. In a franchise about wormholes, warfare, and alien gods, Daniel is the voice asking the most important question: What does it mean to be human — and can we be better? He's not just a Stargate hero — he's its moral foundation.

Teal'c

Teal'c, I saw you stand up to a god. You refused to kill. I saw you make that decision... In that moment I learned everything I needed to know to trust you.

―Jack O'Neill Stargate SG-1

Teal'c is the embodiment of honor, strength, and quiet rebellion. Portrayed by Christopher Judge, Teal'c is a former First Prime of Apophis, who betrays his false god and defects to join the SG-1 team. A man of few words and immense presence, Teal'c brings not only raw combat skill but a deep, often philosophical perspective on freedom, loyalty, and the fight against oppression.

Teal'c was born on the planet Chulak, a world enslaved by the Goa'uld. Raised in a warrior caste, he became First Prime — the highest-ranking Jaffa — to Apophis, one of the most powerful and cruel Goa'uld System Lords. But even as he served Apophis, Teal'c saw through the deception. He secretly harbored doubt, quietly studying the prophecies of freedom and resistance passed down by the Tok'ra and ancient Jaffa elders. When he encountered the SG-1 team on their first off-world mission, he made a choice that would change history: I have seen your world. I have seen the strength of your people. I have chosen freedom. In a single act of defiance, Teal'c turned on his god, freed prisoners, and joined SG-1 — leaving behind his family, his status, and the only life he had ever known.

As a member of SG-1, Teal'c serves as:

  • A warrior and combat specialist
  • A cultural advisor on Jaffa and Goa'uld matters
  • A symbol of the possibility of change

He brings insight into Goa'uld strategy, knowledge of alien worlds, and a deep understanding of honor and sacrifice.

Teal'c is calm, stoic, and deliberate — but far from emotionless. His strength is quiet, measured, and earned. He is deeply loyal to SG-1, especially to O'Neill, whom he considers a brother.

His sense of honor and justice drives his every action:

  • He struggles with guilt over the people he harmed while serving the Goa'uld.
  • He never stops fighting for his people's freedom — even when it puts him at odds with allies.
  • He constantly balances the warrior's code with the path to peace.

And yes — he has a dry, sometimes devastating sense of humor: Indeed.

Teal'c's legacy is monumental:

  • He was the first Jaffa to rebel, lighting the fire of freedom.
  • He helped overthrow the Goa'uld Empire through courage and leadership.
  • He played a central role in the formation of the Free Jaffa Nation.
  • He fought not only physical enemies — but centuries of cultural brainwashing and fear.

In a universe dominated by gods and tyrants, Teal'c chose to become something far more powerful: A free man.

Final Word

Teal'c is more than just a warrior — he is a symbol of liberation. Through him, Stargate SG-1 explored themes of identity, resistance, redemption, and what it means to break free from lies you've been raised to believe. His quiet strength, loyalty, and unshakeable will make him one of the greatest characters in Stargate history — a man who changed not just his people, but the fate of an entire galaxy.

Major General George Hammond

Major General George S. Hammond, portrayed by Don S. Davis, is the commanding officer of Stargate Command (SGC) during the formative years of the Stargate program. A veteran of the U.S. Air Force, Hammond is a leader defined by calm authority, deep compassion, and unshakable integrity.

Hammond takes command of the SGC following the events of the original Stargate film. He is responsible for:

  • Overseeing all SG team operations through the Stargate
  • Managing Earth's response to alien threats (Goa'uld, Replicators, etc.)
  • Balancing military protocols with ethical judgment and diplomacy

Though not a field operative himself, Hammond's decisions often determine the success or failure of missions — and sometimes, the survival of Earth.

Unlike the stereotypical "hard-nosed general," Hammond leads with empathy and trust. He stands up for his people — even against politicians and higher command — and always puts the safety of his teams first. Eventually promoted and reassigned to Homeworld Security, Hammond leaves behind a legacy of honor, loyalty, and stability — the very backbone of Stargate Command.

General Hammond may not carry a weapon into battle, but his presence in Stargate SG-1 is no less heroic. He's the father figure, the steady voice, and the kind of leader every team hopes to have behind them. You have a go. With those simple words, he sent humanity further than it had ever gone — and we trusted him every step of the way.

Ba'al

Ba'al is one of the last and most cunning of the Goa'uld System Lords, portrayed with suave menace by Cliff Simon. Unlike most Goa'uld, who rely on brute force and religious fear, Ba'al uses intellect, manipulation, and charisma to maintain power — making him uniquely dangerous.

  • Serves as main antagonist during SG-1's later seasons and Stargate Continuum.
  • Creates dozens of clones of himself to survive defeat and manipulate galactic politics.
  • Becomes the last major Goa'uld standing long after the System Lords fall.

Ba'al is the perfect late-game villain: smart, unpredictable, and entertaining. Where most Goa'uld were brutes in golden armor, Ba'al was a chessmaster in a silk cloak — proving that brains and ego can be deadlier than any fleet.

Ronon Dex

You've gotta see this guy in action. He is an incredible shot, none of my guys can beat him in a fight, and he's ex-military.

―John Sheppard, Stargate Atlantis

Ronon Dex, portrayed by Jason Momoa, is a former soldier from the planet Sateda and a member of the Atlantis expedition. Stoic, fiercely loyal, and deadly in combat, Ronon is a warrior forged in fire — literally hunted for years across the Pegasus Galaxy by the Wraith before joining Sheppard's team.

After Sateda was destroyed by the Wraith, Ronon was implanted with a tracking device and turned into a Runner — a target for sport. Surviving alone for seven years, Ronon becomes a master of guerrilla tactics, hand-to-hand combat, and survival.When the Atlantis team removes his tracker and offers him a place among them, Ronon accepts — not just for revenge, but for purpose.

He becomes:

  • The team's close-quarters combat expert
  • A fierce protector of civilians and teammates
  • A man searching for home, identity, and redemption

Ronon is intense and quiet, often misunderstood. But beneath the tough exterior is a man who values:

  • Honor in battle
  • Loyalty to friends like Sheppard, Teyla, and McKay
  • A strong sense of justice, especially against the Wraith

His weapon of choice — the energy pistol and sword — reflects his blend of high-tech and primal combat style. In a firefight, you want Ronon on your side. Period.

Ronon Dex is what happens when you take rage, discipline, and loyalty — and give it a mission. He's a walking storm, a relentless protector, and one of the fiercest characters in the Stargate universe. He didn't just join the Atlantis team — he became its spine.

John Sheppard

Lt. Colonel John Sheppard, portrayed by Joe Flanigan, is the charming, unorthodox, and fiercely capable leader of the Atlantis off-world team. With his tousled hair, casual sarcasm, and dead-on aim, Sheppard brings a mix of laid-back cool and deep moral conviction to a role that constantly puts him between life, death, and intergalactic disaster.

Sheppard starts off as a black sheep in the U.S. Air Force, reprimanded for disobeying orders to save lives — the exact kind of guy the Stargate program needs. When he displays a rare ability to use Ancient technology, he's fast-tracked to join the Atlantis expedition. What begins as a support role quickly becomes leadership when Colonel Sumner is killed by the Wraith. Sheppard steps up, not because he wants to, but because no one else can do it better.

Sheppard might joke his way through life-or-death situations, but beneath the wit is a man who:

  • Carries guilt for lives lost under his command.
  • Has a mysterious, isolated past, especially regarding his family.
  • Struggles with what it means to lead, especially when forced to make impossible choices.

Lt. Colonel John Sheppard is the guy who doesn't want to be the hero — but becomes one anyway. He's snarky, smart, loyal, and fearless — and he's always the first through the gate. Sheppard is Stargate Atlantis' wildcard — and its moral core.

Todd The Wraith

“Todd” (not his real name, obviously) is a high-ranking Wraith commander and occasional uneasy ally of the Atlantis expedition. Portrayed with slick menace by Christopher Heyerdahl, Todd stands out as a complex antagonist, walking the tightrope between diplomacy, manipulation, and outright hostility. He's not your average villain — he's the Wraith you can negotiate with… maybe.

Todd is first introduced as a prisoner of the Genii, rescued by John Sheppard in order to escape a common threat. In a surprising twist, he honors the debt — and a strange, recurring “frenemy” relationship is born. Over time, Todd becomes:

  • A diplomatic representative of certain Wraith factions.
  • A strategic partner against shared threats like the Replicators and Michael.
  • A wild card who will save your life or backstab you — depending on the day.

He's the Wraith version of a political operative: smooth, cunning, calculating.

Todd is the gray space between friend and enemy — the dangerous unknown you keep close because he might be useful... and because killing him might start a war. In a galaxy of monsters, Todd stands apart — not because he's less dangerous, but because he's dangerously reasonable.

Nicholas Rush

Dr. Nicholas Rush, portrayed by the brilliant Robert Carlyle, is a Scottish mathematician and astrophysicist assigned to the Icarus Project. Unlike the more idealistic scientists of previous series, Rush is driven by personal pain, ego, and relentless curiosity — making him one of the most complex figures in the Stargate franchise.

When an experiment to dial the 9th Chevron goes catastrophically wrong, Rush and a group of civilians and military personnel are stranded on the Ancient ship Destiny, billions of light-years from Earth. Rush becomes the de facto science lead, but he's far from the voice of reason. He's:

  • Brilliant, but manipulative.
  • Ruthless, but occasionally vulnerable.
  • Obsessed with Destiny's mission — even more than survival.

Rush is haunted by the death of his wife, Gloria, and consumed by the belief that Destiny holds the key to understanding the cosmic origins of life and the universe itself. He believes the mission — the real mission — goes beyond survival or returning to Earth. He clashes frequently with Colonel Everett Young, the ship's military commander, creating a tense brain-vs-brawn power struggle. But make no mistake: Rush often plays chess while everyone else is playing checkers.

Dr. Nicholas Rush is Stargate's most cerebral, most morally gray character — a man who embodies the idea that knowledge has a cost. He may not be liked, but he's essential.Love him or hate him, without Rush, there's no Destiny mission — and no Stargate Universe.

Colonel Everett Young

Colonel Everett Young, portrayed by Louis Ferreira, is the senior military officer stranded aboard the Ancient ship Destiny after the fall of Icarus Base. He's a career soldier, a disciplined man with a strong moral compass — but unlike past Stargate commanders, he didn't volunteer for this. And that makes all the difference.

Young was set to retire from frontline duty to spend more time with his wife, wanting a quieter life after years of service. Instead, he ends up in command of civilians, soldiers, and scientists trapped aboard a ship built for exploration — not survival. The result? A man constantly torn between:

  • Duty and exhaustion
  • Military order and civilian chaos
  • His role as leader and his failing marriage back home

Colonel Everett Young is the realist's hero — a man caught in a job too big, with no good options, trying his best anyway. He doesn't want to be the hero, but when everyone's looking for hope, he stands in front of the fire.In Stargate Universe, where survival is uncertain and morality is blurry, Young is the man who holds it all together — even when he's falling apart inside.

Eli Wallace

Eli Wallace, portrayed by David Blue, is a MIT-level slacker with unmatched potential — a math prodigy plucked from his apartment and dropped aboard an ancient starship billions of light-years from Earth. What starts as a fish-out-of-water story becomes a tale of growth, grief, loyalty, and leadership.

Eli is discovered when he solves a complex mathematical puzzle hidden in an online game — actually a test planted by the Stargate program. Next thing he knows, he's recruited by Dr. Nicholas Rush and thrown into a crisis that lands him aboard Destiny. At first, he's just the tech guy. The awkward kid. The comic relief. But Stargate Universe is about evolution — and Eli evolves into so much more.

Eli starts off inexperienced, unconfident, and a little immature — but his arc is quietly one of the strongest in Stargate history. He grows by:

  • Standing up to Rush, even challenging his mentor when morality is in question.
  • Facing grief, especially after the death of close friends like Riley and Ginn.
  • Taking responsibility, becoming a leader in his own right, not just a sidekick.
  • Showing real emotional intelligence — arguably more than anyone else on Destiny

He's not just the smart guy. He becomes a survivor, strategist, and symbol of hope.

Eli Wallace is the soul of Stargate Universe — a reminder that greatness doesn't always come from rank or training. Sometimes, it comes from a guy in a hoodie who gives a damn. He's us — and he becomes something more.
