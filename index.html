Introduction to Stargate Universe

We're going to be demolecularized, transmitted over two thousand light years through subspace, and then, uh, rematerialized on the other side.

— Samantha Carter, Stargate SG-1

Stargate is a science fiction franchise centered around a mysterious ancient device discovered on Earth: the Stargate. This massive ring-shaped portal allows instantaneous travel between distant planets by creating stable wormholes. First uncovered in Egypt, the Stargate's true purpose remained a secret until Dr. Daniel Jackson, an archaeologist and linguist, decoded its symbols and activated the gateway.

The Stargate technology is far beyond anything humanity has developed. When activated, it dematerializes matter at the atomic level, transmits it through a wormhole, and reconstructs it at another Stargate located on a different world. This breakthrough opens the door to exploring countless alien civilizations, unknown environments, and complex political and military challenges scattered across the galaxy.

The franchise follows the efforts of various teams, including military personnel and scientists, who use the Stargate to explore new worlds, establish alliances, and protect Earth from extraterrestrial threats. The stories blend action, adventure, and mythology with science fiction, creating a unique narrative that appeals to fans of exploration and discovery.

Stargate's enduring popularity lies in its rich universe, compelling characters, and the exciting possibilities that arise from traveling through the Stargate. It challenges humanity to confront the unknown, face new dangers, and expand its horizons beyond the stars. This site is dedicated to exploring all aspects of the Stargate universe, from its technology and lore to its cultural impact.

The History of the Stargate Franchise

The Stargate franchise began with the release of the original movie on October 28, 1994. This film laid the foundation for everything that followed. It sparked three successful television series and two additional movies—though these latter two are more accurately extended episodes rather than traditional cinema releases. Nevertheless, they remain highly regarded by fans.

The original movie, which I will refer to as The Stargate, opens with the discovery of a mysterious circular device covered in ancient symbols, found in Egypt. The artifact was transported to a secure U.S. military facility at Cheyenne Mountain, where extensive research was conducted. Dr. Daniel Jackson eventually cracked the code behind the device, unlocking its true function. We will explore in detail how the Stargate operates and what it enables.

Following the movie's success, three TV series were produced: Stargate SG-1, Stargate Atlantis, and Stargate Universe. Additionally, there are two made-for-TV movies: Stargate: The Ark of Truth and Stargate: Continuum. Beyond these, the franchise also includes an animated series, Stargate Infinity, and a short-run web series titled Stargate Origins.

Stargate SG-1 introduces one of the franchise's central antagonists—the Goa'uld. This parasitic species takes control of human hosts by residing in their throats, suppressing the host's personality while commanding their body. The Goa'uld regard themselves as gods and have enslaved humans through a servant class known as the Jaffa. These Jaffa carry immature Goa'uld symbiotes until they mature, serving as loyal warriors and worshippers. While some Jaffa secretly question the divinity of the Goa'uld, fear of brutal punishment keeps them silent.

The story truly begins when a Jaffa named Teal'c defects and joins the SG-1 team. Over its 10 seasons, SG-1 charts Earth's evolution from an emerging power to a major galactic player, introducing many alien species including the Goa'uld, the advanced Asgard, the mechanical Replicators, the fanatical Ori, and the mysterious Ancients—the original builders of the Stargates.

After the success of SG-1, the franchise expanded with Stargate Atlantis and Stargate Universe. Atlantis focuses on the discovery of the legendary city of Atlantis, a city-ship of the Ancients located in the Pegasus Galaxy. The teams use the Stargate to travel there, a process that demands massive energy. Once in Pegasus, they encounter humans and the Wraith—an insect-like species that feed on human life force, similar to a parasitic bug known as the Iratus bug.

Stargate Universe takes the story even further into deep space aboard the ship Destiny. Built and launched by the Ancients millions of years ago, Destiny was sent to investigate a mysterious pattern detected in cosmic background radiation—an anomaly that suggested an intelligent source. The ship travels from star to star, stopping at planets seeded with Stargates by its sister ships. A unique aspect of Destiny is its ability to harness energy directly from stars, powering its journey through the cosmos as it seeks answers to the universe's deepest mysteries.

Stargate: A Final Word

Stargate is more than just a story about alien technology—it's a gateway to the vast unknown. It challenges us to look beyond our world, to confront the mysteries and dangers that lie among the stars. Through its rich universe and unforgettable characters, Stargate explores humanity's resilience, curiosity, and determination to survive and thrive in a universe far bigger than ourselves. It reminds us that discovery comes with risk, but also with incredible reward. In every journey through the Stargate, we are invited to take that step into the unknown—and see how far we can go.
