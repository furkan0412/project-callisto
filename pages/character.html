Best Stargate Characters Ever

Stargate introduces us many characters but some of them become like a family. We are take a quick look at some of these wonderful characters together in this section. Some characters are good and some are evil but They all effect us in a way.

Jonathan J. O'Neill

He's an amazing man. After everything he's done, he's still modest, quite self effacing actually. He even likes people to think he's not as smart as he really is. Bottom line: He's an incredibly strong leader who's given more of himself for this program than anyone has given for... well... anything I can imagine.

―Samantha Carter, Stargate SG-1

Jonathan "Jack" O'Neill is one of the most iconic and beloved characters in the Stargate franchise. Portrayed by Richard Dean Anderson, O'Neill is known for his dry wit, tactical brilliance, and a deeply human approach to leadership. As the commanding officer of the original SG-1 team, he played a central role in the early missions through the Stargate, often balancing military objectives with moral integrity and compassion.

Jack O'Neill is a decorated U.S. Air Force officer with a background in black ops and deep-space telemetry. Before joining the Stargate Program, O'Neill had retired from active duty following the tragic loss of his young son, Charlie — an event that left him emotionally distant and introspective. His involvement in the original Stargate mission to Abydos in the 1994 Stargate film was meant to be a one-way trip. But the mission's success — and his bond with the alien culture — gave him a renewed sense of purpose.

When Stargate Command was reactivated, O'Neill was brought back to lead SG-1, the flagship team tasked with exploring new worlds, defending Earth, and confronting threats like the Goa'uld and later the Replicators and Ori. While O'Neill maintained a no-nonsense military exterior, his loyalty to his team — Samantha Carter, Daniel Jackson, and Teal'c — became one of the emotional pillars of the series.

O'Neill's leadership style is uniquely his own. He is often sarcastic, irreverent, and even dismissive of complex scientific jargon (much to Daniel and Carter's frustration), but beneath the humor is a deeply strategic thinker and a courageous leader who repeatedly risks his life for his team and for Earth. He isn't afraid to break the rules when necessary — whether it's defying orders to save a life or standing up to alien gods — and he brings a humanitarian touch to the often cold world of military command. His respect for alien cultures, especially his enduring friendship with Teal'c, shows his capacity for empathy and growth.

Final Word

O'Neill eventually rises to the rank of Brigadier General, taking command of Stargate Command before later moving to a more strategic role in Homeworld Security. His influence continues to shape the Stargate Program even after he steps back from active field duty. He represents the soul of SG-1: skeptical but open-minded, tough but compassionate, sarcastic but principled. Jack O'Neill isn't just a soldier — he's the beating heart of Stargate SG-1 and a fan-favorite character whose impact continues to echo across the Stargate universe.

Samantha Carter

Carter, you're one of this country's natural resources, if not national treasures.

―Jonathan J. O'Neill, Stargate SG-1

Colonel Samantha Carter is one of the most accomplished and multidimensional characters in the Stargate universe. Portrayed by Amanda Tapping, Carter is a brilliant astrophysicist, decorated Air Force officer, and key member of the legendary SG-1 team. Balancing intellect, leadership, and compassion, she represents the perfect fusion of science and military discipline in the Stargate program.

Samantha Carter joined the U.S. Air Force at a young age, driven by a passion for astrophysics and aerospace engineering. She earned a Ph.D. in theoretical astrophysics, worked on top-secret research including the Stargate project before its public reactivation, and served in the Gulf War as a combat pilot. By the time she joins SG-1, Carter has already logged over 100 hours in enemy airspace — a fact she reminds O'Neill of during their first mission briefing.

Carter's role in SG-1 is essential. She serves as the science officer, tasked with understanding and manipulating alien technology, including the Stargate itself. But she's far more than a scientist. She's a battle-tested soldier, trained for field combat, capable of tactical decisions under pressure, and one of the team's most dependable operators.

Over the years, Carter plays a vital role in:

  • Defeating the Goa'uld system lords through scientific innovation.
  • Battling and outsmarting the Replicators, including her evil mirror-version, Replicator Carter.
  • Developing Earth's interstellar defense capabilities, including the BC-303 and Daedalus-class ships.
  • Rising to command SG-1 and later the Atlantis Expedition.

Carter is highly analytical, logical, and precise — but she's also deeply empathetic and loyal. She's constantly torn between her scientific curiosity and her military duty, which leads to complex moral dilemmas throughout the series. Her humility, despite being arguably one of Earth's smartest people, is one of her most grounding traits. As a leader, Carter is fair, strategic, and firm when needed. Whether she's commanding SG-1, helping lead the fight against the Ori, or taking charge of Stargate Atlantis, she proves time and again that she can lead with strength, grace, and intelligence.

Her professional bond with Jack O'Neill evolves into a deep — but unspoken — romantic tension, complicated by military regulations. Her relationships with Daniel and Teal'c are built on mutual respect and friendship, forming a family dynamic within SG-1.

Carter's legacy is immense:

  • She helps reverse-engineer Goa'uld and Ancient tech.
  • Plays a vital role in developing Earth's intergalactic space fleet.
  • Acts as a scientific and military ambassador in both the Milky Way and Pegasus galaxies.

Final Word

Colonel Samantha Carter is a trailblazer, not just within the Stargate universe, but in science fiction television as a whole. She's one of the rare characters who can reroute a wormhole, disarm a nuclear bomb, and win a gunfight — all in the same episode. She stands as a symbol of brilliance, bravery, and balance — a hero who proves that being smart doesn't mean being weak, and that leadership comes in many forms. Carter is, without question, one of Stargate's greatest legends.
